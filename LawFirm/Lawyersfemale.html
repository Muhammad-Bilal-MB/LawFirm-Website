Alicia Patrick

LL.B.

The Legum Baccalaureus (LL.B.) is the foreign equivalent of a J.D. It's not available in the U.S. Combined initials. Some law schools have intensive programs for earning a J.D. along with another degree. These include a Ph.D., Master of Social Work (M.S.W), Master of Business Administration (M.B.A) and Medical Degree (M.D.). Attorneys with a combined degree include all of the initials after their name.

Samina Rizwan

J.S.D.

A Doctor of the Science of Law (J.S.D. or S.J.D.) is the equivalent of a Ph.D. in law. Attorneys who get these lawyer initials generally become scholars or teachers of law and social sciences. This is a very specialized degree that's selectively awarded.

Emma Hughes

LL.M.

The Master of Laws (LL.M.) degree is available to people who already have a J.D. It provides advanced legal study and certification in a specific area of law, such as international law, human rights law or intellectual property law. The lawyer abbreviation LL.M. is often obtained by international lawyers who want to earn global credentials.
